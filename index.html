1923

New Zealand Veterinary Association (NZVA) formed with 26 foundation members; Chief Veterinary Officer CV Reakes was the first President; NZVA was active in promoting compulsory veterinary registration based on educational qualifications

1925

Case book of vet in Balclutha shows many calls for calving, metabolic disease, turnip poisoning, lameness and colic in horses

1926

Veterinary Surgeons Act passed; and the Veterinary Surgeons Board was formed for the compulsory registration of veterinarians, including those who had been practising for 10 years or more; 48 veterinary practitioners and 52 qualified veterinary surgeons were registered; veterinarians trained in British and Australian veterinary schools, as well as graduates from Pretoria and Ottawa veterinary schools and others with equivalent training

1928

Melbourne Veterinary School closed
